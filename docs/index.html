How this audit works

What this service does

During every live World Cup match, an automated agent reads the score Google displays in its search results and, in the same moment, the score from an independent reference feed (FotMob, powered by Opta data). It repeats this every 30 seconds.

What is measured

Accuracy: the share of checks where Google's score matched the reference exactly.

Lag: when the sources briefly disagreed (typically right after a goal), how many seconds Google took to catch up.

Score errors: disagreements that persisted beyond 90s, which can no longer be explained by normal feed latency.

How to read the verdict

Green card: accuracy is high and steady. No action.

Amber card: accuracy is trending down or lag is growing. Watch closely.

Red card: a confirmed score error occurred in the last hour, or accuracy dropped below 95%. Raise it with Google's sports data provider with the findings log as evidence.

Methodology: scores are compared as displayed to end users, on a 30-second cycle, during live play only. Disagreements shorter than 90 seconds are classified as feed latency, not errors. The reference feed is independent of Google's data supply chain. This page reloads its data every 60 seconds.
